Alikhan Nurmukhamedovich Bukeikhanov is a prominent public and government figure, organizer and leader of the national democratic party “Alash”, head of the first Kazakh national government of Alashorda, scientist, and talented publicist. Alikhan Bukeikhanov devoted his entire adult life to the struggle for freedom and political independence of the Kazakh people.
Alikhan Bukeikhanov was born on March 5, 1866 in village No.7 of the Tokraun volost of the Karkaraly district of the Semipalatinsk region (now the territory of the Aktogay district of the Karaganda region).

Akhmet Baitursynov (1872 - 1937) was a man who, from childhood, strived for knowledge, and then devoted all his strength to the education of his people. Probably, science was closer to him than politics, but he understood that it was not enough to just teach science, he needed to change people’s lives, so political struggle became an important part of his life. Living at a turning point, Baitursynov never betrayed his principles, even if he knew that it was dangerous.

Akhmet Baitursynov was born on September 5, 1872 in the village of Sarytubek, Zhangeldinsky district, Torgay volost (now Kostanay region). His family came from the Shakshak clan of the Argyn tribe. Father, Baitursyn Shoshakuly, was a descendant of the batyr Umbetai. The mother's name was Kunshi. There is no information about her. We know that she died in 1909, but her son could not come to her funeral. It is known that Akhmet’s father in 1885 resisted the police who began to cause chaos in his native village. The retribution was cruel: Baitursyn and his brothers were exiled to Siberia for 15 years. The boy was only 13, and he had to make his way further in life without his father's help.

Mirzhakyp Dulatov is a major representative of Kazakh culture and literature of the early 20th century, poet, writer, public figure.
His political activity began in 1905. In Omsk, Dulatov met with A. Baitursynov and A. Bokeikhanov. He was one of the authors of the Karkaraly petition. The first collection of poems “Wake up, Kazakh!” published in Kazan in 1909. The entire circulation of the book was destroyed, the author was persecuted and imprisoned. However, Dulatov continued his literary and journalistic activities, translated works of Russian and European classics (A.S. Pushkin, M.Yu. Lermontov, F. Schiller, G. Tukai) into Kazakh. He published in Orenburg the novel “Unhappy Zhamal” (1910), a collection of works “Azamat” (1913), “Terme” (1915). From 1913 he lived in Orenburg, together with A. Baitursynov he published the socio-political newspaper “Kazakh”. In 1916, together with Baitursynov and Bokeykhanov, he opposed the royal decree on conscripting Kazakhs for rear work. He actively participated in organizing the Alash movement and wrote the party program.
